Introduction

Hollywood is one of the oldest film industries in the world but it is not perfect. Gender inequality in Hollywood and the media in general is a long-standing issue, related to the difference in pay between men and women, difference in opportunities and roles, and more. Ageism is another issue in the industry: Hollywood has pushed actresses out as they age, due to the male gaze and gendered beauty standards, which emphasize the notion that women need to look “young” to be desirable and valuable. Meanwhile, most actors do not experience this.


Visualization Goals

As students who love watching films, we wanted to take a closer look at the Hollywood film industry and visualize some of the relationships between film stars, costars, film genre, film year, box office revenue, and more. We aim to convey, at a scale of 40 stars, a narrative about ageism and sexism in Hollywood over time, through our visualizations.


Intended Audience

General public, film enthusiasts, casting directors, production companies, film review boards


Data

We collected data on a subset of popular actors and actresses in the Hollywood film industry. Our data sources were the following:

  • 1. IMDbPY - We extracted data through a Python package called IMDbPY. By calling certain functions from the package, we retrieved information about films, including cast, directors, screenwriters, year of release, and other metadata. We also retrieved information film stars, including their date of birth, their performances, their costars, trivia facts, and more. For reference, documentation of this package can be found on this site.

  • 2. The Numbers: Where Data and the Movie Business Meet - We extracted information about worldwide, domestic, and international box office revenue generated by films on this site.

We then put together three datasets, which we used to create the data visualizations you see on this website:

  1. 1. Stars and Their Costars - This dataset contains information about each star we chose as well as which films they have been in, and the top 5 costars who costarred in those films with them. It also has information about stars’ birthyears and costars' birthyears. *

  2. 2. Film Genres - This dataset contains information about the number of films each star has performed in each genre.

  3. 3. Box Office Revenue - This dataset contains information about each film and how much it made in box office revenue ($).

Viz 1

Once you select a star, the bubble chart in the following dashboard displays film counts by genre for the selected star. Each bubble represents a film genre that the selected star has appeared in. Genres are color coded, as documented in the legend. The size of each bubble represents the number of films in a particular genre the selected actor or actress has starred in throughout their career thus far. For example, Jennifer Aniston has starred in 32 comedy films. Feel free to hover over any bubble. You will be able to determine which genres a star appears in more often.

Once you select a star, the bar chart in the following dashboard displays the films the selected star has been in along with the age difference between them and their lead costar of different gender for each film. The orange bars represent the films in which the selected star was younger than their costar, while the blue bars represent the films in which the selected star was older than their costar. You can hover over each bar to see more information about each film and the stars in it. As you look from top to bottom of the chart, the films are in ascending order chronologically, so you can see how age difference between star and costar changed over time.

Once you select a star, the scatter plot in the following dashboard displays worldwide box office revenue (adjusted for inflation) generated by the films starring the selected star. Each point represents a film the selected star has appeared in. The x axis represents the years the star was in films, while the y axis represents revenue in millions of US dollars. You will be able to observe trends in revenue over time.

It is recommended that you view the visualization in full screen (click the button in the corner on the bottom right of the visualization).

Viz 2

The following bar chart displays yearly total worldwide box office revenue percentage by gender of lead stars. For economic consistency, we have adjusted all the box office revenue values for inflation. Each bar represents a year. Each bar is divided into sections, corresponding to three categories of films: films with male leads, films with female leads, films with male and female leads, and they are color coded, as documented in the legend. The length of each section represents the percentage of total worldwide box office revenue generated by films of the respective category in a particular year.

It is recommended that you view the visualization in full screen.

Viz 3

The following dashboard contains two bubble charts side by side: the chart on the left displays counts of films with female leads by genre; the chart on the right displays counts of films with male leads by genre. Within a chart, once you select a film year using the dropdown, each bubble represents the total number of films with leads of the given gender in a particular genre in the selected year. You will be able to compare film counts between female leads and male leads for each genre.

It is recommended that you view the visualization in full screen.

Viz 4

To get insights out of the following visualization, begin by selecting one or more film star(s) of your choice from the list on the right hand side. Once you select a star, you will immediately see a line plot displaying the total worldwide box office revenue generated by the films they starred in per year. The box office revenue values have been adjusted for inflation based on US dollar value in 2021. Get more information about the film release year, the revenue, and the star by hovering over points. Tom Cruise and Sandra Bullock are pre-selected for you, but if you want to visualize this plot for other stars, feel free to "unselect" them and "select" your choices. You will be able to observe trends over time. Does box office generally tend to grow over the years? How quickly does it change? Do you see noticeable differences between line plots for actors and line plots for actresses?

It is recommended that you view the visualization in full screen.

*Note: Not all films of a specific star are represented in this dataset if a) there are films in which they did not have a costar or b) their costar’s birthyear is unknown at the time of data collection.
